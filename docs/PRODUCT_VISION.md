# Product Vision

## Mission

Help people turn mixed references into original music direction fast.

## Product Summary

`solfege` is an AI-assisted music creation product where the user provides 2-3 references and the system interprets them into a structured creative result.

Those references can include:

- a sonic reference track
- a meme or spoken clip
- a mood or structure reference
- text instructions
- a target BPM or duration

The system should understand:

- style
- energy
- roughness
- emotion
- pacing
- hook direction
- arrangement intent

Then it should produce:

- a music concept
- a prompt package
- track structure guidance
- lyrics or vocal direction
- eventually audio output

## Who It Is For

Primary audiences:

- aspiring producers who need guidance
- artists who want fast concept generation
- remix-minded creators
- underground electronic musicians

The product should work for:

- beginners who want to learn
- intermediate users who want direction
- advanced users who want creative acceleration

## Product Promise

Give the user a strong original track direction from a few references, not just a generic AI output.

## What Makes It Different

- reference-driven workflow
- stronger creative control
- learning-oriented experience
- room for niche and culturally specific energy
- usable even before full long-form generation is perfect

## Product Pillars

### 1. Reference Understanding

The app should be excellent at reading the intent behind mixed references.

### 2. Creative Guidance

The app should teach and guide, not just output content.

### 3. Structured Control

Users should be able to influence BPM, energy, mood, and duration clearly.

### 4. Progressive Generation

Start with structured outputs, then grow into audio generation and iteration.

## Long-Term Vision

Long term, `solfege` can become a full creative environment for:

- concept generation
- prompt generation
- arrangement planning
- preview generation
- song generation
- section editing
- creative iteration

The key is to build toward that in layers instead of trying to solve everything in version 1.
