# Legal Notes

## Purpose

These notes define the early legal and safety boundaries for `solfege`.

They are not legal advice.

They are product-planning guardrails.

## Highest-Risk Areas

- copyrighted songs used as references
- YouTube links and downloaded media
- meme clips involving public figures
- impersonation or voice-cloning behavior
- defamatory or abusive outputs

## Safer Early Positioning

Frame the product around:

- inspiration
- analysis
- creative transformation
- original output

Avoid framing it around:

- copying artists exactly
- cloning public figures
- recreating copyrighted songs

## Early Product Rules

- references are for interpretation and creative direction
- exported output should be positioned as original generation
- do not market the product as a tool to copy an artist 1:1
- do not promise legal use of ripped third-party content

## Public Figure / Meme Risk

Bulgarian meme and politician material may be creatively interesting, but it adds risk.

Risks include:

- likeness concerns
- defamation concerns
- moderation concerns
- platform enforcement

This area should be treated carefully from the start.

## Input Policy Questions To Resolve Later

- Will the app fetch media from links directly, or only accept user-uploaded content?
- Will it store reference content permanently or only metadata?
- Will it support public sharing of generated works?
- Will it allow user voice references?

## Recommended Early Safety Boundaries

- allow references, but keep rights expectations conservative
- avoid voice-cloning features in early versions
- avoid artist-name mimicry as a primary feature
- avoid direct downloads from unsupported sources until policy is clear
- build basic moderation rules before opening broadly

## Product Messaging Rule

The app should be described as helping users generate original music direction from mixed references, not as a system for copying existing songs or personalities.
